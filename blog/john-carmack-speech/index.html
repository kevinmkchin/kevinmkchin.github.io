John Carmack UMKC Commencement Speech

November 30, 2021

“There are lots of viable paths through engineering. The path I advocate for is ‘knowledge in depth’ - for knowing things really deeply. It’s true that the modern wizardry of building amazing applications by bashing together a bunch of JavaScript libraries and web services has indeed minted a lot of startup millionaires, but it does remind me of Mickey Mouse as the sorcerer’s apprentice in Fantasia animating broomsticks with magic he doesn’t really understand and eventually making a really big mess. Knowing how to pull the levers of power is rewarding, but there’s a satisfaction in really understanding how they work and knowing things very deeply. There’s also a great confidence you can have when you know that you could build something from scratch even if you choose not to. Almost everything that we work with has really great depths to explore. Now you can’t know everything, but you should look at this as you can know anything. Anything that you turn your attention to that you choose to focus on you can understand. Nothing is magic, and you can tear things apart if you need to to find the details.”
